## 4. Slice Summary: `jb` Client 0

The `jb` slice used a bounded pool of 15 candidates and accepted 14 Evidence
Units. It tested the locked question of which finance-career avatar, pain
wedge, copy angle, pricing/package, and workflow bet had the strongest public
market pull.

The strongest valid signal was not generic finance-course demand. The slice
found directional support for a high-finance recruiting workflow bet centered
on aspiring IB analyst or internship candidates who need networking,
resume/story credibility, technical reasoning, modeling practice, and
mock-interview reps.

The slice kept PE recruiting as a `Probe` variant and kept broad analyst, FP&A,
and personal financial advisor lanes unresolved rather than rejected.

The slice's Action Ceiling was `Test`. It did not support `Move` or `Commit`
because vendor pages dominated pricing and package evidence, forum evidence was
self-selected and anecdotal, labor-market evidence was contextual rather than
product-demand evidence, and external willingness to pay remained unvalidated.

This was useful decision support because it narrowed the first `jb` test shape
while preserving uncertainty and alternatives. It did not prove external buyer
demand or that `jb` generalizes commercially.

## 5. Slice Summary: SH-01 Shadow Satellite

The SH-01 slice stayed inside one buyer-visible narrative pressure decision:
whether Zendesk should monitor, defend, reposition, or test a response to
Intercom Fin's AI-agent-for-customer-service narrative.

The slice showed buyer-visible pressure from Intercom/Fin's repeated AI-agent
narrative, direct Zendesk comparison, Fin-on-Zendesk positioning, outcome
pricing, and Zendesk's own Resolution Platform, AI-agent, Forethought, and
comparison responses.

The strongest valid use was actor strategy, buyer-visible comparison pressure,
positioning pressure, and pricing or measurement objection hypotheses. The
slice did not establish costly buyer behavior, Zendesk buyer switching, budget
movement, or independent pull caused by Intercom Fin.

The Action Ceiling was `Test`. The slice recommended testing a reversible
competitive-response message around resolution with control, quality
validation, workflow depth, and predictable operating model. It explicitly
rejected unsupported claims that Zendesk beats Fin on resolution rate, that
vendor benchmarks are independently true, or that direct comparison pages prove
buyer demand.

This was useful portability evidence because SH-01 did not become broad
competitor monitoring and did not require a new competitor-intelligence
primitive. It used the same Core Spine primitives to keep actor strategy,
buyer-belief pressure, and demand apart.

## 6. Slice Summary: BT2-04 Backtest

The BT2-04 slice replayed Shutterstock's possible response to generative image
AI using public evidence visible on or before the 2022-09-15 cutoff. It
explicitly separated the pre-cutoff pool and at-cutoff replay memo from the
post-window outcome pool.

The at-cutoff evidence supported public technology availability, commercial
creative-tool visibility, Shutterstock marketplace exposure, preexisting
AI/data posture, and visible contributor/IP risk. It did not establish
Shutterstock customer switching, direct revenue pressure, or buyer demand for
AI-generated stock.

The at-cutoff Action Ceiling was `Test`, paired with `Hold`. The replay would
have recommended probing partner and data-licensing paths, testing a controlled
AI-generation or AI-assisted creative workflow with rights/provenance gates,
and holding unmanaged AI-generated contributor uploads until rights, labeling,
and library-quality controls were defined.

The post-window comparison found the replay directionally useful and
underconfident. The later Shutterstock partnership, AI Image Generator,
expanded data agreement, and contributor-fund posture matched the controlled
partner/data-licensing path, but that later similarity was not used to raise
the at-cutoff ceiling.

This was the strongest slice for leakage discipline and outcome calibration.
It preserved post-window exclusion and kept later outcome similarity from
retroactively strengthening the pre-cutoff record.

## 9. Signal Integrity Behavior

Signal Integrity was not decorative.

Across the slices, integrity labels changed discounting, uncertainty, exclusion
state, Decision Strength, or Action Ceiling:

- In `jb`, vendor claims were used for pricing/package, actor strategy, copy,
  and workflow evidence, not independent demand. Forum material supported pain
  and objections, not market size or conversion. BLS data supplied market
  context, not product demand.
- In SH-01, vendor resolution-rate, automation-rate, comparison, customer-story,
  logo, and outcome-pricing claims were discounted. They supported actor

- In BT2-04, technology availability and incumbent asset fit supported `Probe`
  or bounded `Test`, while unresolved legal, contributor, provenance, and buyer
  demand uncertainty capped the ceiling and created a paired `Hold`.

The repeated behavior is important: Core Spine used integrity analysis to lower
action strength even where the surface narrative looked persuasive.

## 14. What This Proof Run Showed

The dominant pattern across this bounded proof run was controlled downgrading.

Across contexts, strong-looking public material was often vendor-authored,
campaign-shaped, source-limited, volatile, or missing costly buyer behavior.
The primitive chain still produced useful decisions because it preserved what
the signal could validly inform and capped unsupported action.

Boundary note:

These are observations from this bounded proof run. They are not updates to the
Core Spine product contract, a data-spine schema, a feature plan, or an
implementation plan.

Specific lessons:

- A noisy Client 0 market can still produce a usable `Test` memo when offer
  architecture, workflow pain, objections, and counterevidence are kept apart.
- A competitor narrative case can stay bounded when actor strategy and
  buyer-visible pressure are separated from buyer pull.
- A backtest can be useful without claiming the later outcome was obvious.
- `Test` can be a strong proof-run output when it is the correct ceiling.
- Evidence limitations are product signal, not just caveats, because they shape
  the recommendation and the next uncertainty to resolve.
