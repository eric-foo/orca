## Review Purpose

Adversarially test whether the patch gives clean future agents enough durable instruction to preserve and clean engagement context without letting engagement become Judgment, proof, or scoring.

Attack at least these failure modes:
- Capture fails to preserve source-visible metrics, order, hierarchy, timing, missingness, or row-to-metric binding.
- Projection or Cleaning accidentally interpret engagement as credibility, independence, artificial amplification, demand, Signal Use, Decision Strength, Action Ceiling, Commit/Scale, or score boost.
- Data Lake placement is wrong: engagement context should be Bronze when raw, Silver when mechanically normalized or cleaned, Pre-gold only as mechanical alert candidate, Gold-ready only as bounded assembled evidence with `judgment_status: not_evaluated`, and Gold only after Judgment.
- CSB or Scanning turns high engagement into proof instead of routing attention, source-preservation asks, or venue prioritization.
- The engagement registry becomes an invented central authority that conflicts with local spine authorities, or its DCP overclaims validation/enforcement.
- The patch misses a controlling doc needed for clean agents in this lane, or adds an unnecessary doc surface that increases future confusion.
- The patch fails the user-owned distinction: 23 upvotes supporting X can carry more cleaning/navigation weight than 0-upvote contrary noise, but it cannot settle market truth by itself.
- The patch smuggles runtime enforcement or data-lake schema implementation even though the current authorization is docs-only.
