## Judgment

Do not accept the full S-04 state package as offered unless no credible
alternative remains before the budget deadline.

The city should accept the pension-reform principle, including moving new hires
to the Wisconsin Retirement System, but should counteroffer against the broader
state governance shackles bundled with the revenue authority.

## Counterposition

The city should offer a narrower fiscal-discipline package:

1. Accept WRS for new hires.
2. Create a dedicated pension lockbox for legacy pension obligations.
3. Adopt a binding pension funding schedule.
4. Ban unfunded pension benefit expansions.
5. Accept narrow fiscal oversight through public reporting, independent
   actuarial review, and state visibility into pension funding.
6. Use service-level commitments and reporting instead of rigid public-safety
   staffing mandates.
7. Add a sunset or step-down once pension funding targets are met.
8. Enforce a structural-balance rule: no recurring spending built on one-time
   money after the bridge period.
9. Run an independent waste/procurement audit covering overtime, contracts,
   procurement, vacancies, fleet, facilities, subsidies, and administrative
   duplication.
10. Include a controlled correction mechanism for pension underfunding.

## Controlled Correction Mechanism

If the city misses the pension funding schedule for controllable reasons beyond
a defined tolerance band, automatic corrections should apply in the next budget
cycle.

Acceptable corrections include increasing the pension-lockbox share of new
revenue, requiring a remediation plan, restricting new recurring commitments
until the city returns to schedule, requiring independent actuarial
recertification, and requiring a public vote for any deviation.

The correction mechanism should not punish the city for certified external
shocks, including recession, major revenue underperformance, state aid cuts,
emergency costs, legal judgments, or independent actuarial assumption changes.

## Near-Term Route

Use temporary relief as bridge capital while pursuing pension reform and an
alternative revenue/funding stack. Avoid high-risk bridge tools such as
pension-obligation debt, reserve depletion as a recurring solution, capital
deferrals disguised as savings, or one-time asset sales used to fund recurring
operations.

Hold broad service redesign in reserve because it is slow, politically costly,
and may reduce negotiation leverage before the state package must be accepted
or rejected.

## Rationale

The full S-04 package appears to combine a potentially useful pension reform
with broader state-imposed governance constraints. WRS may be a good structural
move, but accepting unrelated shackles would trade away local fiscal discretion
before the city has tested a cleaner reform-and-revenue alternative.

The counteroffer is intended to prove that the city is not avoiding discipline:
it accepts pension reform, dedicated funding, transparency, correction
mechanisms, and structural balance. The rejected part is overbroad control, not
reform.
