## D1 — Company name and domain

**Forseti** is the company name; **forsetihq.com** is the company domain.

- Owner words (2026-07-02): "we have decided (not acquired yet) forsetihq.com
  and Forseti as company name"; "im getting forsetihq.com first".
- The owner acquires the domain personally; agents never execute purchases or
  unattended account creations (boundary carried in the acquisition handoff).
- The name is unscreened at ratification; the trademark/collision screen
  (acquisition lane, step 1) gates all further brand spend.
- "Orca" remains the internal project/repo codename indefinitely; renaming
  repo artifacts to Forseti is explicitly out of scope and unwanted.

## D2 — Brand architecture: one company, endorsed sub-brand

One legal company (Forseti) carries the evidence-backed consumer-market
decision-intelligence thesis. The Creator Signal carve-out becomes a **thin,
endorsed sub-brand on its own website** ("<sub-brand> by Forseti").

- forsetihq.com carries the company and the standard: decision-intelligence
  positioning, the provenance/judgment story, and (when authorized) published
  zero-spoiler backtest receipts. The creator vetting surface never lives
  there — the thesis anti-positions against feeds/dashboards ("a calibrated
  decision, never a feed") while the vetting product is a browsable
  filter/sort/compare surface; one site cannot carry both without muddying
  the anti-positioning.
- The sub-brand site is the scene-native front for an authenticity-driven
  niche community, endorsed visibly "by Forseti" so trust flows upward.
- Each brand's assets (domain, handles) stay separately owned and cleanly
  separable.
- Ratification receipt: recommended 2026-07-02 with this rationale; owner
  accepted the design in the same exchange ("it makes sense") and directed
  the action translation plus the acquisition lane spin-off consistent with
  it. Owner selected the holding + waitlist posture (D6) as its near-term
  depth.

## D3 — Carve depth: brand/product-level only

No legal entity split now. Owner asked for a recommendation ("recommend
actually - this one i have no idea"), the recommendation was brand-level
only, and the owner replied "1 - agree" (2026-07-02).

- Grounds: the creator registry sits on the shared Orca capture/lake spine;
  a split today forces substrate duplication or inter-entity IP/data
  agreements before any customer exists.
- Entity-split triggers (any one re-opens the question, owner decides):
  acquisition interest in the creator line; outside funding raised
  specifically for it; a liability/compliance reason.
- Standing obligations that keep a future split cheap: keep the ratified
  seams clean (Capture owns the registry; Creator Signal owns the surface;
  the customer claim-projection is the boundary) and keep brand assets
  separable (D2).

## D4 — Carve-out long-run role: keep both open

The creator product is designed so it can serve as the entry wedge into the
broader Forseti decision-intelligence relationship AND survive as a
standalone line or acquisition asset. Owner selected "Keep both open"
(2026-07-02 decision questionnaire).

- The upsell asset, when it matures, is the decision-intelligence output
  class (demand reads, memos/decks with action ceilings) — packaged as
  decided answers with evidence, never as access to internal machinery such
  as the Commission Signal Board.
- Sequencing stays foundation-first per the carve-out architecture's
  Direction Update v0.1; nothing here pulls the customer product forward.

## D5 — Vertical-scope doctrine: generic engine, vertical-deep product, one brand per market world

Owner-ratified 2026-07-02 ("do the decision stuff i guess" on the
recommendation below). Three layers with different answers:

1. **Engine/schema: generic.** Vertical-neutral schema with vertical
   specificity as data — already the ratified rule in the carve-out
   architecture; restated here as the company-design layer it supports.
2. **Product and data: vertical-deep, one vertical at a time.** The moat is
   niche-complete, longitudinal, provenance-backed coverage of a vertical.
   Generic creator intelligence is the crowded incumbent category
   (horizontal discovery databases); the premium the sizing memo's hinge
   depends on exists only inside a vertical. This grounds in
   moat/positioning, not current data volume — per owner direction
   (2026-07-02: "please ignore the 33 profiles for now, its not even created
   yet"), present profile counts and the not-yet-built customer product are
   irrelevant to this doctrine.
3. **Brand: one creator-intelligence brand per market world; verticals are
   coverage editions under it.** The beauty world (fragrance → skincare →
   beauty) is one buyer world: same indie/DTC operator class, same specialist
   agencies, overlapping creators, one compounding evidence graph. Fragrance
   is the first edition badge ("for fragrance"), not a separate brand. A
   second creator brand becomes a real question only on entering a different
   market world entirely (different buyers, agencies, scene); the neutral
   schema keeps that open, and the decision is deferred to that day.

Consequence for naming: the sub-brand name must comfortably cover beauty,
not merely stretch from fragrance.

## D7 — Sub-brand working name: Aphrodite

Owner-proposed 2026-07-02 ("how about Aphrodite"). Status at first
ratification: **WORKING NAME** pending screen — amended same day to
**ADOPTED WORKING NAME with a light-check-then-buy posture** (see the D7
Amendment below, which governs).

- Fit: satisfies D5's naming consequence (beauty-world-wide, not
  fragrance-locked), memorable, consumer-credible; endorsement form
  "Aphrodite by Forseti".
- Named risk (load-bearing for the screen): a crowded namespace in beauty
  commerce — cosmetics/skincare brands named Aphrodite exist, and the word is
  a famous mythological name with attendant discoverability and clearance
  questions. A live in-repo datapoint: our own capture set already contains a
  fragrance product named Aphrodite ("Soki London Aphrodite",
  `docs/review-inputs/youtube_shorts_fragrance_tone_expansion200_capture_v0.json`,
  observed 2026-07-02). The relevant classes for this product are
  software/business intelligence (35/42), not cosmetics (class 3), which
  helps but is not clearance; the screen must specifically check
  analytics/influencer-software uses.
- Gate: the acquisition lane's second tranche (buying the sub-brand's domain
  and handles) must not fire until (a) the screen clears this name and (b)
  the owner confirms it post-screen. This tightens the trigger already named
  in the acquisition handoff packet. [SUPERSEDED by the 2026-07-02 amendment
  below — the hard gate is replaced by a light-check-then-buy posture.]

## D7 Amendment (2026-07-02) — name confirmed, gate relaxed

Owner reconsidered the D7 gate in the same session ("wait i thought for sub
name we can literally use anything since the big name is what matters"),
weighed the mythology-consistency question ("should the mythologies be from
same origin? e.g. forseti from... norse?") including the Norse-system
alternatives (Freyja, Idunn), and confirmed the name: "lets jsut do
aphrodite then."

- **Name: Aphrodite, confirmed as the adopted working name.** Mixed
  mythology (Greek sub-brand under the Norse company name) is accepted as a
  non-issue — the sub-brand's job is instant scene legibility, which
  Aphrodite wins; a single-pantheon naming system was considered and
  declined.
- **Gate relaxed.** Rationale adopted: under the endorsed thin-sub-brand
  architecture (D2), sub-brand equity is deliberately low and a pre-launch
  rename is cheap by design, so trademark exposure — a legal question, not a
  brand-equity question — scales with public commercial use, which is gated
  far away (D6 holding + waitlist; foundation-first sequencing). The D7 hard
  gate is replaced with:
  1. a **light collision sanity check** (software/analytics/influencer-tool
     uses of "Aphrodite") before any asset spend on the name;
  2. **formal trademark clearance deferred** to the first public commercial
     use under the name (Vetting-v0-or-later, itself owner-gated);
  3. pre-launch rename acknowledged as the cheap fallback if either check
     turns something up.
- The Soki London Aphrodite capture datapoint is downgraded from gate
  justification to background context (it is a class-3 cosmetics product,
  not a software collision).
- **Unchanged:** the Forseti screen (D1) remains step 1 of the acquisition
  lane and still gates company brand spend — the company name is on the
  domain, email, eventual contracts, and every endorsement line, so its
  rigor is not relaxed.
