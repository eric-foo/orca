## Case + cutoff (for orientation)

- Case: batch-1 #4 / repeatability "case #2" -- DTC skincare brand **Topicals**
  (founded 2020 by Olamide Olowe and Claudia Teng; DTC launch 2020-08-07 online
  + Nordstrom) expands from direct-to-consumer into Sephora retail distribution
  at ~month 9 of the brand's life.
- Cutoff (anti-leakage): **<= 2021-03-15**, pre-announcement of the Sephora
  expansion (public press clusters ~2021-03-23..03-29). All blind evidence is
  bounded to this date.

## What actually happened after the cutoff (the sealed result)

Facts below are post-cutoff and dated. Sourced from public press; figures are as
reported (company-disclosed growth multiples are self-reported, not audited).

- **The Sephora expansion proceeded.** Topicals' Sephora entry went live in
  2021 (Sephora.com via the Sephora Accelerate BIPOC-brand program; "sold out
  within 48 hours" per multiple 2021 interviews), and the brand extended into
  Sephora US store distribution. It became positioned as a core Sephora skincare
  brand rather than a brief pilot.
- **Reported commercial trajectory:** brand sales **tripled (3x) in 2021**;
  the company **projected revenue growth of >600% for 2022**; by 2022 it was
  described (by the company and trade press) as **"the fastest-growing skincare
  brand at Sephora,"** reportedly selling "one product every minute" in 2022.
- **Funding after the expansion:** on **2022-11-10**, Topicals announced a
  **$10M Series A led by CAVU Consumer Partners** (Jenna Jackson to the board),
  bringing **total funding to ~$15M**; the round was earmarked for omnichannel
  growth, new hires, channel expansion, and brand marketing. Investor base
  included Marcy Venture Partners plus celebrity backers (e.g. Gabrielle Union,
  Kelly Rowland). Later reporting cites total funding raised growing beyond this
  (figures up to ~$22.6M reported by 2024-2025).
- **Longer-run trajectory:** the brand continued to scale and expand
  distribution internationally -- Sephora Canada, then **Sephora UK (entered
  ~April 2024** with reformulated products) -- and reached a five-year
  anniversary (2025) still operating as an established Sephora skincare brand
  (e.g. a 2025 anniversary "Acne Reset Kit" launch). No public reporting of the
  Sephora retail expansion being reversed, pulled, or failing.

## Net (one line, sealed)

The month-9 DTC->Sephora retail expansion proceeded and the brand scaled
strongly on the back of it through 2021-2022 and beyond, with institutional
follow-on funding. (Self-reported growth multiples; not audited.)
