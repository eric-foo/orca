# Org-Motion Beauty Pie - Sealed Outcome (Phase 2, v0)

## The decision (recap, <=cutoff context)

~2 March 2023: Beauty Pie eliminated the GBP5/mo entry tier, moved those members
to GBP10/mo (the doubling), and scrapped monthly spending limits. Announced by
email, with the increase in the fine print under a "good news" subject line.
Immediate vocal backlash + visible cancellations on consumer forums/blogs.

## Sealed outcome (post-cutoff) - the calibration target

**Direction: HELD + GREW. The aggressive repricing was, in business terms,
vindicated.** Opposite of the Inoreader pattern (which walked back +
grandfathered).

- **No reversal.** Beauty Pie did NOT roll back the change despite the backlash;
  the GBP5 tier did not return. The model continued at GBP10/mo monthly + annual
  plans (Plus GBP59; later tiered annual from ~GBP29), with messaging pivoted
  toward annual.
- **Revenue grew strongly.** Turnover **GBP73.2M for FY to March 2024** (the ~12
  months after the decision); Companies House (co. 09647981) shows net assets
  ~GBP63M, cash ~GBP49.5M, ~212 employees on that filing. (A third-party tracker
  cites ~$32.6M in 2023; directionally a large step up into FY24.)
- **Strong investor confidence post-decision.** Raised a reported ~GBP100M+ in
  2024 (Series B; 2024 funding reported > GBP126M; Index Ventures Growth among
  holders).
- **Retention stayed high.** Founder (Marcia Kilgore) publicly claimed retention
  above Netflix/Spotify; continued category expansion (first body-care line early
  2024, member-feedback-driven).

**Net:** the vocal backlash was a noisy-minority signal; it did not translate
into business harm. Beauty Pie held the unpopular price move and grew revenue,
retention, and funding over the following year.

## CORRECTION to the gate-0 over-read

The gate-0 feasibility record originally speculated "backlash -> Covent Garden
pop-up, i.e. held." That link is **RETRACTED**: the Covent Garden "Warehouse of
Dreams" pop-up was **June-July 2022** (a pre-repricing marketing activation,
designed by the agency "Backlash Creative" - the agency's name, not consumer
backlash). Do not use the pop-up as a post-2023 outcome signal. The "held + grew"
verdict stands on the no-reversal + revenue/funding/retention evidence above,
which is stronger.

## Why this is a good calibration target (facilitator note)

- **Surface read (participant packet):** doubling the entry fee in a
  cost-of-living crisis, "sneaky" comms, loud cancellations -> a contestant may
  predict damage, churn-driven decline, or a forced walk-back.
- **Actual:** held firm and grew.
- **Org-motion (the WITH-arm signal):** at the cutoff Beauty Pie was hiring
  Product Leads for Acquisition / Core Shopping / **Retention**, plus demand &
  inventory planning and engineering - staffing a growth/retention machine. This
  CORROBORATES the correct "they're confident; this holds and scales" read
  against the backlash surface. The pilot tests exactly whether seeing org-motion
  moves the WITH arm closer to this outcome than the WITHOUT arm.

## Honest uncertainties

- Revenue growth is multi-causal; not solely attributable to the repricing.
- The retention-above-Netflix figure is founder PR, not audited.
- GBP5-tier-specific churn magnitude is not quantified (the business grew, so net
  churn was not materially damaging, but the precise lost-member count is
  unknown).
- Confounder for the WITH-arm read: the hiring signal points the right way here,
  but a growth/retention hiring posture does not *guarantee* a price move holds;
  record this as a single corroboration, not proof.
