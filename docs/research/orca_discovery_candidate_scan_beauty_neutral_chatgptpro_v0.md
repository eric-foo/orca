## Verification Plan

Run the no-contact scan against the top three openings before owner sign-off on
any niche selection.

1. Define the three openings tightly:
   fragrance mini/discovery/layering/mist allocation; scalp-as-skincare routine
   allocation; barrier-first/derm-developed skincare allocation.
2. Build candidate pools:
   30-50 fragrance contexts, 20-30 scalp contexts, and 20-30 barrier-first
   skincare contexts.
3. For each candidate, record:
   named operator, role, current public decision trigger, decision family,
   public source origins, costly-behavior evidence, integrity risks,
   private-data dependency, and whether the likely output is a memo/deck rather
   than a feed/dashboard.
4. Apply the live buyer-proof gate:
   floor = at least one gradeable costly-behavior instance in a qualifying
   demand-origin family; material action ceiling = at least two converging
   effectively independent origins; retail presence is G4 org-motion
   corroboration, not a G1 independent-origin count.
5. Promote a first verification target only if it yields at least 8-12 strong
   or tentative candidate contexts, including at least 4 strong contexts, across
   repeatable decision families without paid, private, hidden, proprietary, or
   absurd-risk sources.

Kill or downgrade fragrance if its best contexts reduce to one viral scent, one
creator cluster, one PR/trade launch chain, acquired/too-scaled brands, weak
costly behavior, or a dashboard/feed need. Move hair/scalp above fragrance if
it produces more named operator-visible live retail decisions with cleaner
routine evidence and lower claims drag. Move skincare above both only if the
barrier-first slots show cleaner 30-90 day decisions without needing private
efficacy or formulation evidence.
