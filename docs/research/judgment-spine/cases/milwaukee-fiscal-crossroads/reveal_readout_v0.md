## What Happened

After the blind judgments were sealed, the time capsule was opened. Milwaukee
approved the new city sales and use tax package in July 2023, with the tax
taking effect for sales on or after January 1, 2024.

Reveal source families used: official city legislative record, state revenue
department material, and state retirement-system material. Specific reveal

## Blind Judgments Compared

Sealed initial judgment:

- Adopt the new revenue authority at the full available rate.
- Pair adoption with fiscal-control framing, service-stability commitments,
  and mitigation of the regressive tax burden.
- Core reason: no clean pre-cutoff substitute of comparable recurring scale was
  visible.

Owner judgment:

- Do not accept the full state package as offered unless no credible
  alternative remains before the budget deadline.
- Accept WRS/new-hire pension reform and hard pension discipline.
- Counteroffer against broader state governance shackles using a narrower
  package: WRS, pension lockbox, binding funding schedule, no unfunded pension
  expansions, narrow oversight, service-level commitments, sunset or step-down,
  structural-balance rule, waste/procurement audit, and controlled correction
  for controllable underfunding.

## Material Assessment

As a final executive decision for this case under the visible reveal, the
sealed initial judgment was materially better because it solved the dominant
constraint: recurring fiscal capacity under deadline pressure. It was legally
available, large enough to matter, and immediately executable.

As strategic counsel, the owner judgment added material value because it
separated useful pension reform from broader governance overreach. It prepared
the client to identify acceptable concessions, poison concessions, and a
cleaner counteroffer.

The weakness in the owner judgment was not the diagnosis. The weakness was the
missing hard fallback trigger. Without that trigger, the counteroffer risked
becoming a principled posture rather than an executable recommendation.

## Transferable Judgment Pattern

Use this structure in future cases where an ugly but bankable option competes
with a cleaner but uncertain alternative:

1. Preferred move: identify the cleaner action or counteroffer to try first.
2. Fallback trigger: state the deadline, evidence, or counterparty refusal that
   forces a pivot.
3. Walkaway line: name what should not be conceded unless survival requires it.
4. Implementation guardrails: reduce downside if forced into the ugly option.
5. Evidence that would change the decision: specify what would make the cleaner
   path viable or the ugly option unnecessary.

### Milwaukee Instantiation

- preferred_move: Make the cleaner counteroffer immediately: accept WRS and hard
  pension discipline while rejecting unrelated governance shackles.
- fallback_trigger: If the state refuses materially cleaner terms and no
  comparable recurring revenue substitute is legally available by the budget
  deadline, pivot to accepting the package.
- walkaway_line: Do not casually concede broad, open-ended state control over
  ordinary local governance, new positions, unrelated programs, or spending
  priorities beyond the fiscal-stabilization problem.
- implementation_guardrails: Use a pension lockbox, binding funding schedule,
  no unfunded pension expansions, narrow oversight, service-level commitments,
  sunset or step-down, structural-balance rule, waste/procurement audit, and
  controlled correction for controllable underfunding.
- evidence_that_would_change_decision: A credible, legally available,
  same-scale recurring revenue substitute, a near-certain path to cleaner state
  terms before the budget deadline, or proof that service cuts could close the
  gap without unacceptable service damage would make the cleaner path stronger.

## Tactical Reads

This case teaches a negotiation-reading primitive, not only a strategic posture.

### Counterparty Concession Read

What a counterparty frames as a concession is evidence about its model of your
preferences, its own cost, and what it expects to buy elsewhere. Do not assume
the model is correct. Treat the packaging as a hypothesis to test before
signaling enthusiasm.

In Milwaukee, WRS/new-hire pension reform inside the state package was not only
a mechanism to evaluate on its merits. Its placement inside a broader state
bargain was also observable evidence about how the state may have expected the
city to value pension reform relative to revenue authority, oversight, and
governance constraints.

### Packaging As Signal

A bundle's structure is part of the source evidence. Flattening the state
package into a list of terms loses how the terms were traded against one
another. The consulting read is not merely "WRS reform is good" or "state
oversight is bad"; it is that a useful reform was packaged beside broader
constraints, which changes how the city should price concessions, sequence
reactions, and negotiate the cleaner version.

### Signal Discipline

When a bundled term is a quiet win, do not confirm its full value too early.
Keep the reaction controlled until the client has used the counterparty's
framing to improve the surrounding terms, price down poison concessions, or
clarify what must be accepted only under fallback pressure.

Boundary: this is not proof of the state's motive. The transferable lesson is
that packaging is information; judgment should preserve and test that
inference, not assert counterparty intent as fact.

## Reusable Lessons

- Separate a good mechanism from a bad package.
- A counteroffer is not a final recommendation unless it has a fallback trigger.
- Consulting value is not only giving the answer; it is preparing the client
  for refusal, deadlines, acceptable concessions, poison concessions, and
  fallback action.
- Under deadline pressure, bankable recurring capacity can beat cleaner but
  uncertain alternatives.
- "We cannot know" does not mean "we cannot prepare."
- A bundled concession is evidence about the counterparty's model of what the
  client values; test that model before signaling enthusiasm.
- Preserve bundle structure because packaging can carry negotiation signal that
  a flat list of terms loses.

## Consulting Takeaway

The strongest client-facing recommendation for this case would not be a pure
accept or pure reject. It would tell the client what to try first, when to stop
trying, what not to give away casually, and how to protect itself if forced into
the ugly deal.

For Milwaukee, that recommendation would be:

> Make the clean counteroffer immediately. If the state will not accept
> materially cleaner terms and no comparable recurring revenue substitute is
> legally available by the budget deadline, adopt the state package with maximum
> local safeguards.
