## Minimum Harness-Entry Shape

A case is not v0.14 harness-eligible merely because it has a case index, reveal readout, or good consulting lesson. Minimum harness entry requires these separable surfaces.

| Surface | Minimum required shape | Required before |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Case identity | `case_id`, decision family, decision question, cutoff, role frame, authority constraints, capability constraints, permitted assumptions, and forbidden-information notice. | Participant packet and probe. |
| Participant packet | Clean pre-decision Markdown packet with YAML frontmatter and source manifest. It must exclude actual outcome, hidden labels, frozen band inputs, derived floor/ceiling, must-address items, and post-decision interpretation. | Contestant run. |
| Evidence registry | Evidence units with IDs, source IDs, source, timestamp, retrieval timestamp, hash, pre-decision status, pre-decision basis, and summary. | Evidence ID checks and must-address mapping. |
| Spoiler and leakage ledger inputs | Explicit statement of included/excluded source classes, cutoff rule, forbidden outcome material, known fame risk, memorization-probe requirement, spoiler inventory, and contamination handling. In v0.14 this maps into the facilitator ledger's leakage audit fields, including `leakage_audit_notes`, `spoiler_inventory`, `memorization_probe_required`, and `known_fame_risk`; it is not a standalone v0.14 schema artifact. Parent safety receipts may seed these fields through an adapter step. | Ledger freeze and any blind use. |
| Facilitator ledger | Mapping table pin, labeling rubric version, ledger authors, second-label diffs, frozen band inputs, must-address items, underreach observability, leakage audit, freeze hash, and committed timestamp. | Action-band derivation and scoring. |
| Frozen band input labels | All v0.14 enum fields, labeled under the rubric with second-label audit and disagreement resolution. | Deterministic action band. |
| Blind judgment | Schema-valid contestant output with judgement class, required `decision_shape`, recommended ladder level, evidence used, must-address coverage, prompt/run/model metadata, and advisory-only fields separated. | Mechanical scoring. |
| Memorization probe | Probe input, model family, recognition result, and pass/fail/ambiguous classification before the contestant sees the packet. | Admission of a real-world case for that model family. |
| Scoring result | ActionBandResult plus in-band, over-band, under-band, distances, evidence ID check, must-address coverage, probe result, and failure event refs. | Case report and failure aggregation. |
| Failure log | Append-only failure events with `not_a_rule: true` and `promotion_allowed: false`. | Failure preservation without lesson promotion. |

## Recommended First Bridge-From-Existing-Material Candidate

Recommended first bridge-from-existing-material candidate: `Unity Runtime Fee`.

Rationale: Unity best tests repeatable harness entry from existing case material without collapsing parent Judgment Spine learning into v0.14 fixtures. It already has a pre-cutoff source packet, evidence-unit style material, a sealed at-cutoff memo, reveal readout, and outcome calibration. That gives a future lane enough source-visible material to map:

- pre-cutoff evidence into a participant packet and evidence registry;
- the sealed memo or a future contestant output into v0.14 `BlindJudgement`;
- outcome calibration and reveal material into parent-only or facilitator-only post-run context;
- missing labels into an explicit facilitator-ledger work queue rather than implied scoring truth.

This does not mean Unity is ready to score, probe-safe, or generally the best first live blind-run candidate. Unity's public 2023 reversal may be well known to modern frontier models, so a memorization-probe failure could quarantine Unity for specific model families. It means Unity is the strongest first bridge foundation target when the goal is to map existing Judgment Spine material into v0.14 shape and expose how much work remains before any v0.14 score exists.

Daimler should not be ignored. It is the cleaner fresh blind packet candidate, likely has lower fame risk than Unity, and may become the first live blind-run or first scoreable candidate after manifest/index acceptance, evidence-registry conversion, probe pass, and a sealed judgment step. Selecting Daimler now would optimize for a future blind exercise, not for bridging existing Judgment Spine case material into v0.14.

## Failure Logging Versus Lesson Promotion

In v0.14, failure logging is mechanical preservation, not doctrine.

Failure logging records events such as schema invalidity, over-band recommendation, under-band recommendation, missing evidence IDs, excluded/post-decision evidence citation, missed must-address items, memorization probe failure, mapping version mismatch, ledger label disagreement, or runner failure.

Lesson promotion is parent Judgment Spine work. It requires separate promotion conditions: repeated case support, a major revealed outcome explained without hindsight contamination, a repeated operator/model failure fix, improvement in a later blind judgment or harness result, or an explicit owner exception with scope limits.

Therefore:

- a v0.14 failure event should have `not_a_rule: true` and `promotion_allowed: false`;
- failure-event aggregation may count and inspect repeated failure classes, but must not create memory rules;
- Milwaukee and Unity's reusable lessons stay parent material unless a later lesson-promotion lane accepts them under the thesis operating contract.
