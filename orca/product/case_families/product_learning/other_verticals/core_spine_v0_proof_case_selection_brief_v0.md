## 2. Selection Problem

Core Spine v0 needs two locked proof inputs before evidence work can begin:

1. one exact buyer-visible competitor narrative pressure decision for the
   shadow-satellite memo dry run; and
2. one exact historical backtest case with a cutoff date, case-selection owner
   or decision source, anti-cherry-pick rationale, and post-window exclusion
   rule.

The selection must happen before evidence interpretation. The proof case should
not be chosen because known evidence looks favorable, because the later outcome
is persuasive, or because the case is convenient for a marketing story.

The required read set defines the target shapes but does not supply exact
candidate cases. Therefore this brief can define the rules and identify the
blocked inputs, but it cannot select real-world cases without either
owner-supplied candidates or explicit lightweight discovery authorization.

## 3. Selection Rules

These rules are defined before evaluating candidates.

### Shadow Decision Rules

A shadow-satellite candidate is selectable only if it satisfies all rules:

- It is one exact buyer-visible competitor narrative pressure decision.
- It names a clear decision owner or decision context.
- It names a buyer-visible belief or consequence.
- It is relevant to competitor narrative response, not general market watching.
- It does not become broad competitor monitoring.
- It does not require new Core Spine primitives.
- It does not import finance-career assumptions from `jb`.
- It can plausibly be inspected later through public, market-level evidence
  without inspecting that evidence now.
- It can use the allowed verbs `Watch`, `Probe`, `Test`, `Hold`, or `Move`.
- It can define costly behavior in the selected domain before interpretation.
- It can preserve source-family admissibility and blind spots without creating
  exact source maps in this brief.

### Backtest Rules

A historical backtest candidate is selectable only if it satisfies all rules:

- It is one historical case with a later outcome known.
- It has a cutoff date before the outcome was obvious.
- It plausibly had public pre-cutoff visibility that can be inspected later
  without inspecting evidence now.
- It is relevant to Core Spine's decision-evidence standard: Decision Frame,
  Evidence Unit, Signal Integrity, Signal Use Classification, Decision
  Strength, Action Ceiling, Decision Memo, Backtesting and Outcome Memory, and
  Boundary Rules.
- It names a case-selection owner or decision source.
- It includes an anti-cherry-pick rationale recorded before evidence
  interpretation.
- It preserves fail, blocked, inconclusive, overconfident, underconfident,
  early, late, wrong, and useful outcomes as calibration input.
- It excludes post-window evidence from the at-cutoff recommendation,
  Decision Strength, Action Ceiling, and backtest judgment.
- It does not become a marketing demo unless separately labeled and kept out of
  internal calibration unless leakage controls pass.
