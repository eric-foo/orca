## Core Spine v0 Proof Question

Can the same eight Core Spine primitives turn noisy public evidence into
inspectable allocation recommendations across `jb` Client 0 and at least one
materially different non-`jb` satellite?

The first proof packet must test decision discipline, evidence inspectability,
signal-quality judgment, and portability. It must not treat polished memo
writing, source volume, or hindsight narrative quality as proof.

## Shared Evidence And Inference Standard

The first proof packet must use the same eight Core Spine primitives across the
`jb` Client 0 brief, the competitor narrative response brief, and the backtest
pre-registration note.

| Primitive | Preparation requirement |
| --- | --- |
| Decision Frame | Name the allocation question, decision owner or context, consequence, allowed recommendation verbs, success criteria, kill criteria, and what the frame does not prove. |
| Evidence Unit (EvidenceUnit) | Prepare for later inspectable units with source or source-family basis, timestamp, capture/access timing, excerpt or observed pattern, raw claim, provenance, transformation history, relevance, inclusion state, and source limitations. Do not collect evidence in this artifact. |
| Signal Integrity | Require visible integrity labels and effect statements. Integrity must change discounting, uncertainty, exclusion, Decision Strength, or Action Ceiling. |
| Signal Use Classification | Classify what a signal may validly inform: demand, attention, resonance, positioning, objection, distribution, buyer belief, actor strategy, manipulation risk, weak evidence, or exclusion. |
| Decision Strength | Map evidence quality to qualitative action threshold by weighing audience fit, costly behavior, independence, integrity, counterevidence, alternatives, consequence, and reversibility. |
| Decision Memo | Prepare memo boundaries only. Later memos must include recommendation, evidence basis, alternatives, uncertainty, kill criteria, update triggers, and what would change the answer. |
| Backtesting and Outcome Memory | Pre-register one historical case-selection policy and cutoff discipline before interpretation. Preserve misses, overconfidence, underconfidence, and inconclusive results as calibration input. |
| Boundary Rules | Keep work public, market-level, non-deceptive, docs-first, and non-runtime. Block private, intrusive, deceptive, post-window-leaked, or `jb`-authority-leaking evidence. |

Demand must not be inferred from engagement by default. Engagement can support
attention, distribution, actor strategy, buyer belief, manipulation risk, or
resonance without proving demand. Recommendation verbs must not exceed the
Action Ceiling supported by later evidence.

## Client 0 `jb` Decision-Frame Brief

### Decision Question

Which finance-career avatar, pain wedge, copy angle, pricing/package, and
workflow bet has the strongest public market pull?

### Decision Context

`jb` is Orca's first internal Client 0. It is used to validate method
usefulness, data-spine pressure, signal-quality judgment, and decision-memo
usefulness. It does not validate external willingness to pay.

### Consequence

The decision matters because weak evidence could push `jb` toward the wrong
avatar, pain wedge, copy angle, pricing/package, or workflow bet. Overclaiming
attention as demand would create false confidence; underusing valid public
signals would leave useful allocation evidence on the table.

### Allowed Recommendation Verbs

Allowed recommendation verbs are constrained by the qualitative Action Ceiling:
`Watch`, `Probe`, `Test`, `Hold`, `Move`, and only where later evidence
strictly satisfies the Core Spine `Commit` preconditions, `Commit`.

The first proof packet should expect lower-ceiling verbs unless later evidence
shows clean-enough integrity, independence, audience fit, costly behavior,
counterevidence handling, and material consequence. No recommendation may use
`Commit` from engagement alone.

### Method-Usefulness Success Criteria

The `jb` brief is useful if later proof work can:

- distinguish attention, resonance, objection, and demand claims;
- compare `jb` options through inspectable Evidence Units;
- name source limitations and blind spots without hiding uncertainty;
- show how Signal Integrity changes Decision Strength or Action Ceiling;
- produce recommendation language that a skeptical reader can reconstruct from
  evidence and inference;
- preserve update triggers and kill criteria.

### Kill Criteria

The `jb` frame should be killed or blocked for this proof if:

- `jb` categories, mechanics, paths, templates, lifecycle rules, validation
  habits, or success logic become Core Spine authority;
- internal method validation is overclaimed as external willingness to pay;
- costly behavior cannot be defined for the finance-career context;
- recommendation verbs exceed the Action Ceiling;
- evidence cannot separate public market pull from incentive-driven or copied
  engagement;
- source blind spots make the conclusion non-inspectable.

### Costly Behavior Standard

Costly behavior in the `jb` decision context should be named before later proof
interpretation. Candidate examples include payment, serious application or job
search effort, repeated portfolio or resume work, time-intensive interview
preparation, switching from another tool or workflow, sustained workaround
creation, or repeated complaints with stakes.

These examples do not become Core Spine authority. They only define the
Client 0 decision context for later proof review.

### Source Blind Spots And Evidence Limits

The `jb` brief must preserve blind spots around platform incentives, copied
career advice language, aspirational comments, creator or affiliate incentives,
missing private conversion data, non-public employer behavior, and ambiguity
between curiosity, anxiety, status signaling, and willingness to pay.

## Competitor Narrative Response Shadow-Satellite Brief

### Narrow Frame

This satellite is one buyer-visible narrative pressure decision, not broad
competitor monitoring.

### Decision Question

Should the decision owner monitor, defend, reposition, or test a response to a
competitor narrative that appears to be gaining market traction?

### Decision Context

The decision owner is the team accountable for product, positioning, growth, or
competitive response in a market where a competitor narrative may affect buyer
belief or buyer behavior. The buyer-visible belief or consequence must be named
before later proof interpretation.

### Admissibility Requirements And Blind Spots

Later evidence is admissible only when it is public, inspectable, relevant to
the decision frame, and capable of supporting a bounded inference about buyer
belief, actor strategy, distribution, narrative traction, or manipulation risk.
For cutoff-sensitive work, source visibility before the cutoff must be
established.

Blind spots must remain visible, including paid or coordinated campaigns,
affiliate incentives, copied language, analyst-only visibility, platform
sampling bias, weak audience fit, artificial amplification risk, and ambiguity
between competitor intent and buyer pull.

### Relevance Rules

Relevant signals must connect to at least one of:

- buyer-visible belief or consequence;
- competitor actor strategy;
- repeated narrative language with independence assessed;
- objection, repositioning, or defense pressure;
- costly buyer behavior or credible precursor to such behavior;
- manipulation or artificial-amplification risk that changes the response
  ceiling.

The satellite may not add new Core Spine primitives. It must use Signal Use
Classification and Decision Strength to handle competitive intelligence rather
than creating a separate competitor-monitoring core.

### Allowed Recommendation Verbs

Allowed verbs are `Watch`, `Probe`, `Test`, `Hold`, or `Move`.

`Commit` is not expected from first-pass engagement evidence alone. A stronger
commitment would require later explicit authority and evidence beyond noisy
engagement, including clean integrity, independence, buyer fit, costly
behavior, counterevidence handling, and material consequence.

### Costly Behavior Standard

Costly behavior for this satellite means buyer or market behavior that creates
real response pressure, such as switching consideration, repeated buyer
objections, changed evaluation criteria, public comparison with stakes, sales
cycle friction, willingness to test alternatives, or customer-facing pressure
that persists beyond a campaign burst.

### Success Criteria

The shadow satellite succeeds for preparation if it can later test non-`jb`
portability by using the same eight Core Spine primitives, preserving
buyer-visible consequence, and constraining action to the evidence-supported
Action Ceiling.

### Kill Criteria

The shadow satellite is killed or blocked if:

- it becomes broad competitor monitoring;
- it cannot name a buyer-visible belief or consequence;
- it requires new Core Spine primitives;
- finance-career assumptions leak into the frame;
- copied, campaign-driven, or bot-like evidence is treated as independent
  buyer pull;
- action thresholds or costly behavior require owner input that is not
  available.

## Backtest Case-Selection And Pre-Registration Note

The first proof packet requires one historical case (Case) to be pre-registered before
evidence interpretation.

The pre-registration record must include:

- case-selection policy;
- case-selection owner or decision source;
- decision context;
- why the case is relevant to Core Spine v0;
- why the case was not selected merely because the later outcome (Outcome) is persuasive;
- cutoff date;
- why the cutoff is fair and before the outcome was obvious;
- pre-cutoff source visibility requirement;
- post-window evidence exclusion rule;
- allowed result states;
- calibration lesson field.

The selection policy, cutoff date, and post-window exclusion rule must be
recorded before any evidence interpretation. Post-window evidence must be
listed separately and excluded from the at-cutoff judgment.

Misses, overconfidence, underconfidence, early calls, late calls, wrong calls,
useful caution, and inconclusive results remain calibration input. Cherry-picked
marketing demos do not train the internal evidence standard unless they pass
leakage controls.

No historical case is selected or executed in this preparation artifact.
