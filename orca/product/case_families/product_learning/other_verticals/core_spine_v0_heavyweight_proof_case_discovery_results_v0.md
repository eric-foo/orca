## 2. Discovery Boundary Actually Used

The discovery pass asked only eligibility questions:

- does a candidate exist;
- what is the candidate name;
- what market or domain does it belong to;
- what is the rough public timeline;
- is the candidate buyer-visible or historically bounded;
- is there a plausible decision owner or decision context;
- is public inspection later plausible;
- is there a plausible cutoff date or cutoff window;
- what inclusion risks or leakage risks are visible before proof work.

The pass did not ask whether any signal was strong, whether a narrative had
traction, whether buyers changed behavior, what Core Spine would recommend,
whether a backtest would pass, or whether the outcome proves anything.

The pass used official company pages and a small number of public news pages
as eligibility reference checks. It did not inspect comments, reviews, threads,
customer evidence sets, analyst reports in depth, financial models, source
maps, or pre-cutoff evidence collections.
