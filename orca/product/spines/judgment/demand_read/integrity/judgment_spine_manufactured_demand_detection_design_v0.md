## Why This Exists (the differentiator, and the gap it fills)

The thesis names the integrity axis as **decision-critical in its own right** — "identifying
manufactured demand is the contamination check that protects both real reads, not merely an
avoid filter" (`orca_product_thesis_consumer_demand_v0.md`, 2026-06-14 amendment). The
cleaning↔judgment discipline note names distinguishing **independent corroboration from
artificial amplification** as a **core competency** that lives in **Judgment-Spine Signal
Integrity** and is "a judgment competency to be sharpened over cases, not a fixed threshold"
(`core_spine_v0_corroboration_vs_amplification_discipline_v0.md`). The taxonomy names the
**Integrity layer (Signal Layer 5)** and the read type, and the engagement registry holds a
**seed pattern table** (bot-like activity, copied phrase, burst-after-prompt, affiliate push).

What **none** of them specifies is the detection competency itself: *which observable public
tells exist, by what mechanism, which hypothesis each favours, how they compose into a
read without becoming a score, and how that read gates a Scale-grade commitment.* C2 Rule 3
(R3d) explicitly leaves a **core-owned hole**: "Each governed risk carries a present-fingerprint
+ an absent-clearing-check… **Core owns the requirement, shape, and per-class families; the
vertical deck owns the specific tells**." This artifact fills that hole. It is the engine that
lets a demand read confidently separate real demand from fake or hollow demand — **low
false-positive** (don't commit into a manufactured spike) **and low false-negative** (don't let
a hype layer scare you off a genuine breakout).

## The Frame: three buckets, and the one hard call

The read separates three states of an apparent demand surge:

| # | Bucket | What is real | What is not | Detectability |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | **Outright fake** | nothing | the accounts, the reviews, the followers are fabricated | Easiest — fabrication leaves the most public tells |
| 2 | **Amplified-but-hollow** (the dangerous middle) | the **spend** on amplification is real | the **demand** it manufactures has weak/no independent repeat or costly behavior | Hardest — real money buys a real-looking surface |
| 3 | **Genuine-with-amplification** | real **costly behavior** (sellouts, repeat, switching, effortful UGC) under the hype | nothing — the amplification is marketing *on top of* a real demand engine | Hard — the amplification masks the real engine |

**The hard, valuable job is (2) vs (3).** Both carry an amplified layer on top; the question is
whether **real costly behavior survives underneath it.** Getting (2)-vs-(3) right is exactly the
thesis's two-sided promise: avoid manufactured spikes (kill the false positive on bucket 2) AND
confidently capture genuine breakouts under hype (kill the false negative on bucket 3).
Bucket 1 is the cheap case the gross filter mostly catches; buckets 2 and 3 are where the
differentiator is earned. **The discrimination is: detect real costly behavior UNDER the
amplification** — which is the corroboration-vs-amplification discipline applied to a live surge.

## The Bayesian-Likelihood Frame (qualitative — INV-1 holds)

Detection is treated as a **likelihood** problem, used as a **reasoning frame, never a
computation**:

- A **tell** is an observable public signal that is **more expected under a manufactured
  hypothesis than under an organic one** (or vice versa). Each tell carries a **qualitative
  likelihood direction** (favours-manufactured / favours-organic / neutral) and a **qualitative
  strength** (weak / moderate / strong tell) — stated in words, justified per-case.
- Tells **stack** into a **qualitative posterior disposition**, not a number. The read states
  which hypothesis the *body* of tells favours and **why**, citing the load-bearing tells. This
  is the same "interpretation, not arithmetic" posture by which INV-1 is enforced elsewhere
  (C2 contract, item 2).
- **INV-1 guard (the tempting crossing point).** No prior, likelihood, or posterior is ever a
  number, fraction, ratio, threshold, or table. There is **no manufactured-risk score**, no
  weighted sum of tells, no count-of-tells cutoff. "Stack the tells" means *reason over the
  body of evidence*, not *add up points*. A read that computes a manufactured score from tells
  fails INV-1 exactly as a computed C2 weight would.
- **Independence is the truth-amount, not volume** (carried from the corroboration-vs-amplification
  discipline and C2's de-correlation sub-step). Ten tells that all sit inside one coordinated
  layer are one tell. The posterior is driven by **independent convergence across mechanism
  classes and venue families**, never by how loud a single layer is.

The qualitative posterior maps onto the **three evidentiary states C2 Rule 3 already defines**,
so this design hands C2 a disposition it can already act on:

| Posterior disposition | C2 Rule 3 state | C2/C3 effect (consumed, not re-derived here) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Tells meet a stated **sufficiency bar** → manufactured | **R3(a) confirmed-present** | **Cap / defeater** — withhold the positive verdict on that dimension; no real demand to weight |
| Suspicious tells, **no clearing-check run**, not dispositive | **R3(b) unconfirmed** | **Ceiling discount, reversibility-banded** — least-recoverable dimension binds; advise the recoverable path until checked |
| **Clearing-checks pass** (or risk not applicable) | **R3(c) confirmed-absent** | **Neutral baseline, never positive credit** (anti-gaming; AR-05) |

## Tell Catalogue (CORE — organized by manufactured-mechanism class)

Core owns the **mechanism classes**, the **present-fingerprint / absent-clearing-check shape**,
and the **evasion** column (the arms-race). The **specific venue tells** (e.g. exact Sephora /
TikTok-Shop / Reddit fingerprints for beauty) are **satellite** — owned by the beauty deck
(`beauty_venue_card_set_v0.md`), not minted here. Several rows **organize, by mechanism, the
existing engagement-registry patterns** (bot-like activity, copied phrase, burst-after-prompt,
affiliate push, reward-driven comments) — this catalogue references that table, it does not
duplicate it.

Each class states: **present-fingerprint** (tells that, if seen, favour manufactured — the R3d
present-fingerprint), **absent-clearing-check** (the observation that would clear the risk — the
R3d clearing-check, each needing a stated *sufficiency bar* owned per vertical), **evasion** (how
a determined amplifier defeats the tell), and the **bucket** it most implicates. Each tell's
**reliability tier** (near-dispositive → weak-alone) and its **documented exemplars** are folded
in §"What the External Research Adds" (2026-06-20) — they calibrate how much weight a tell earns
without changing the classes here.

### Class A — Fabricated accounts / bots  (bucket 1; cheap to detect, cheap to partially evade)
- **Present-fingerprint:** thin/young account histories; identical or drip-synchronized timing
  clusters; unnatural follower/engagement ratios; audience irrelevant to the niche; burst-then-silence.
- **Clearing-check:** account-history depth and longevity; organic cross-source discussion by the
  same handles; downstream *behavior* (clicks, search, purchase) not just posting.
- **Evasion:** aged/purchased accounts; residential proxies; LLM-varied text to defeat
  copied-phrase tells; "drip" timing to defeat burst tells.
- **Maps to:** engagement registry → *Bot-like activity*.

### Class B — Review-stuffing / fake reviews (incl. insider / staged-independence)  (bucket 1 → 2; review surfaces)
- **Present-fingerprint:** review-velocity spike **decoupled from independent traffic/sales**;
  templated or near-duplicate language; **a 5★ launch wall that later decays to 1★** (the post-campaign *shift* — **not** a static bimodal *shape*, which genuinely polarizing products produce without manufacturing); **shared-reviewer
  / co-review network overlap** (the same small pool across unrelated products — empirically the
  *strongest* public-data tell, beats text; see §"What the External Research Adds"); incentivized-
  without-disclosure. **Insider / staged-independence** sub-type: employees/managers posting as
  ordinary customers (e.g. employee-written reviews under false identities; staffed "random shopper"
  testimonials) — the opinion-holder may be real but the *independence* is fabricated.
- **Clearing-check:** verified-purchase share; review cadence **tracking** independent traffic;
  longitudinal, varied reviewer histories; for insider risk, identity/relationship independence
  from the brand.
- **Evasion:** verified-purchase fraud (buy-then-refund); staggered seeding to flatten the
  velocity spike; partitioning reviewer pools to defeat the network tell; **paid genuine-purchase
  reviews** (real money, real purchase, paid opinion — largely undetectable, see Limits). Note the
  strongest insider/fake-review evidence is usually a **whistleblower or reimbursement record**, not
  a star-pattern (evidentiary hierarchy, Tier 1).

### Class C — Paid amplification / coordinated promotion  (bucket 2 — THE dangerous middle)
- **Present-fingerprint:** **promotion-engagement mismatch** (a creator promotes X but engagement
  runs *below their own baseline* — the taxonomy's named divergence tell); synchronized
  multi-account posting; disclosure gaps (no `#ad` on obviously seeded content); creator-prompt
  bursts with **short half-life**; visible ad-spend while organic search/UGC stays flat.
- **Clearing-check:** **persistence after the paid push ends**; independent (non-seeded) costly
  behavior; search-interest lift that **outlives** the campaign window.
- **Evasion:** undisclosed paid; micro-influencer **swarms** engineered to look organic;
  whitelisting / dark-posting (ads that don't appear on the creator's grid); **seed-then-real**
  (a paid push that *ignites* genuine demand — this is the (2)→(3) transition the strip-test below
  exists to catch).
- **Maps to:** engagement registry → *Affiliate push*, *Burst after creator/brand prompt*,
  *Like/comment spike*, *Reward-driven comments*.

### Class D — Coordinated origination laundering  (bucket 2; "manufactured consensus")
- **Present-fingerprint:** a claim/trend whose **root receipt traces to a single coordinated
  origin** then echoes as if independent (copied phrasing, syndication, SEO copying); trade-press
  laundering of one PR seed; "everyone is saying" that maps back to one brief.
- **Clearing-check:** **original-source mapping** (does it trace to one origin?); varied phrasing
  from **unrelated** sources; use by the actual target audience, not just relayers.
- **Evasion:** brief-driven creator scripts with deliberately *varied* wording; paid
  "organic-looking" UGC seeded across venues.
- **Maps to:** engagement registry → *Copied phrase*, *Competitor campaign repetition*. Contrast
  the **wind-caller** case (taxonomy Layer 2): a *legitimate* origination hub (e.g. INCIDecoder's
  commissioned SPF tests becoming a root receipt) is **independent corroboration with a single
  origin**, not laundering — the discriminator is whether the origin is *coordinated/paid* vs
  *independent and costly*.

### Class E — Channel-stuffing: sell-in masquerading as sell-through  (bucket 2; supply-side)
- **Present-fingerprint:** retail-placement / restock signals (**sell-IN** — the brand pushed
  product onto shelves) **without** corresponding **sell-THROUGH** (consumers buying *off* the
  shelves).
- **Clearing-check:** sell-through evidence — velocity, restock pressure **driven by demand**,
  not by the brand's own placement.
- **Evasion:** hard to fake publicly, but **genuinely ambiguous**: sell-in can precede real
  demand. **Doctrine note (carried from C2 Rule 3):** "Channel sell-in ≠ sell-through is an
  **owner judgment call** (integrity-artifact vs transient), **not auto-capped**." This class
  routes to the owner, it does not fire R3(a) by itself.

### Class F — Dupe-wave / comparison distortion  (context-dependent — can be real OR manufactured)
- **Present-fingerprint (manufactured side):** comparison-bait engineered by a brand/competitor;
  coordinated "dupe" framing with no independent switching.
- **Clearing-check (real side):** **price-driven rerouting with stated cause** — dupe adoption,
  trade-down, switching that is *costly behavior* (per the taxonomy's pricing refinement, "one of
  the most legible demand signals there is" in beauty).
- **Evasion / ambiguity:** a real dupe wave and a manufactured one look alike at the surface;
  only independent switching behavior separates them. Often **transient**, not manufactured —
  route a real-but-decaying dupe surge to C3 as transient, **not** to a manufactured cap.

## The (2)-vs-(3) Discrimination Method (the heart — detect real costly behavior under the amplification)

This is the method the whole differentiator turns on. Amplification on top **does not negate**
real demand underneath; the read must **look below the amplified layer** for independent costly
behavior. It is the buyer-proof **manufactured-defeater** made into a procedure ("if the only
costly signal sits inside the same coordinated layer that divergence reveals… likely
manufactured/coordinated").

1. **Identify the amplified layer.** Mark which signals sit **inside** a coordinated/paid layer
   (seeded posts, prompted bursts, the brand's own placement, laundered echoes) vs **outside** it.
   This requires the cleaning spine's **non-destructive** preservation of source identity,
   independence, and cluster structure — if dedup flattened that upstream, the layer can't be
   stripped and the discrimination is impossible (carried from the discipline note's "Trap").
2. **Strip the amplified layer.** Set the inside-layer signals aside (they are real *spend*, not
   real *demand*) and ask what survives.
3. **Test for independent costly behavior outside the layer.** The decisive evidence: sellouts /
   restock pressure **not** driven by the brand; **repeat** purchase; **switching with stated
   cause**; effortful UGC from **non-seeded** accounts; **persistence after the push ends**.
   (Engagement Quality Ladder levels 7–8: *Persistence* and *Costly behavior* — the registry's
   own top rungs.)
4. **Call it:**
   - **Survives → (3) genuine-with-amplification.** The amplification is marketing on top of a
     real engine. Hand C2/C3 a **real** disposition; the read proceeds on the persistence axis
     (transient by default, durable earned by observed persistence). **The false negative is
     avoided** — hype did not bury a real breakout.
   - **Nothing survives → (2) amplified-but-hollow.** Manufactured disposition → R3(a) cap →
     discount/avoid. **The false positive is avoided** — real spend did not buy a real read.
   - **Ambiguous → unconfirmed (R3b).** Reversibility-banded discount; advise the recoverable path
     until a clearing-check is **run** (not assumed).

The cost-asymmetry is why this works and survives the arms-race: **faking the surface is cheap;
faking independent costly behavior across multiple independent venue families simultaneously is
expensive** — so the read anchors on the expensive-to-fake thing, not the cheap-to-fake surface.
(Empirically backed by the Amazon fake-review cohort — see the section directly below.)

## What the External Research Adds (folded 2026-06-20)

The external research report (evidence-only;
`docs/research/manufactured-demand/orca_manufactured_demand_beauty_external_research_v0.md`)
corroborates this design and sharpens it. It is `product_learning` **context, not product
authority**; its citations are not independently verified in this lane. Four load-bearing adds:

- **Real, but prevalence unknown — and "hidden promotion ≠ hollow demand."** Manufactured demand
  in beauty is documented at **high confidence** (regulator actions, internal instructions,
  admissions, a peer-reviewed fake-review study where Beauty & Personal Care was the largest
  category). But **no defensible base rate exists**: every sample (regulator sweeps, the Amazon
  study) is enforcement- or group-selected, with no population denominator — an ACCC beauty
  subsample flagged 12/16, but that is not a 75% market rate. The report's own caution **is** the
  design's: hidden promotion proves *the origin of attention was concealed, not that every buyer
  was inauthentic* — which is exactly the (2)-vs-(3) call. **Consequence:** the read classifies a
  *candidate* by convergent tells; it asserts **no** category-wide manufactured rate and carries
  **no** base-rate prior into a case (INV-1-consistent — a prior-as-number would be a score).

- **Evidentiary hierarchy (reliability ordering over the catalogue — CORE).** Tells are **not
  equal evidence**; the qualitative likelihood-strength a tell earns follows this order, and it is
  *qualitative weight, not a score*:
  1. **Near-dispositive (Tier 1):** internal documents / whistleblowers, payment or reimbursement
     records, broker ads matched to the product, regulator findings, platform enforcement.
  2. **Strong public-data (Tier 2):** a **dense shared-reviewer / co-review network** plus timing
     + text anomalies — in direct-ground-truth research, **network features outperformed
     review-text features**, and the network is **costlier to evade** than text.
  3. **Moderate (Tier 3):** synchronized posting, disclosure gaps, post-campaign deterioration.
  4. **Weak alone (Tier 4):** follower count, engagement rate, a viral hashtag, a sellout, a 5★
     average, a single suspicious review.
  A **Tier-4 tell never carries a manufactured disposition alone**; convergence and tier are
  **reasoned, not summed** (INV-1 holds — the hierarchy informs the qualitative read, it is not a
  weighted scoring table).

- **Empirical backing for the strip-test ((2)-vs-(3)).** The Amazon fake-review cohort is the
  clearest documented **amplified-but-hollow** case: manipulation produced **real but temporary**
  rating/review/sales lift that **reverted toward pre-campaign levels within weeks**, after which
  the **one-star share rose** — exactly "peel the layer; nothing durable survives." The report's
  practical rule mirrors the outburst-gate almost verbatim: **require ≥2 independent persistence
  signals before calling heavily-promoted demand durable — "an analytical safeguard, not a
  validated statistical cutoff"** (the design's ≥2-origin + INV-1 framing, independently arrived
  at). Persistence, price integrity, repeat behavior, and cross-venue confirmation are the four
  named separators — the same costly-behavior-under-the-layer the strip-test looks for.

- **Documented exemplars (mechanism → case; beauty-specific tells are satellite).** Concrete
  instances of the catalogue's classes, preserved in the research artifact for the beauty deck:
  **Class B insider/fake reviews** — *Sunday Riley* (employees posting Sephora reviews under false
  identities; surfaced by a whistleblower, not a star-pattern → Tier-1 evidence); **Class A
  fabricated indicators** — *Devumi* (a fake-follower/engagement market — enabling infrastructure;
  no beauty client implied); **Class C undisclosed promotion** — *Teami*, *Charlotte Tilbury/Eliza
  Batten*, *THG/The Fragrance Shop* (affiliate links without an upfront "Ad" label); **insider /
  staged-independence** — *Hismile* (employees presented as random shoppers, caught by identity
  matching). These are **satellite exemplars** — the beauty deck owns the per-venue tells; the core
  spine is unchanged.

## Composition into a Qualitative Manufactured/Real Read

Putting the frame, the catalogue, and the strip-test together, a read:

1. **Attaches integrity labels per venue at C1** — artificial-amplification risk, incentive
   distortion, copied/coordinated — *before* any signal enters the read (buyer-proof packet:
   "every venue's evidence carries integrity labels before it may enter the read").
2. **For each governed manufactured-mechanism risk, runs the discriminator companion** from the
   catalogue: present-fingerprint seen? clearing-check run and passed? neither → inconclusive.
3. **Stacks the tells qualitatively** (Bayesian frame, no number), weighing **independence** and
   the **strip-test**, into a posterior disposition: real / unconfirmed / manufactured.
4. **Emits the disposition in the state vocabulary C2 Rule 3 consumes** (confirmed-present /
   unconfirmed / confirmed-absent), with the **reasoning trace** carrying the load-bearing tells,
   the clearing-checks run, and the (2)-vs-(3) call. A disposition that moves without a cited tell
   or a run clearing-check is non-compliant — the same trace obligation INV-1 is enforced by
   elsewhere.
5. **C3 consumes "real" and never reopens it.** Per the C3 contract, the manufactured axis is
   settled upstream; C3 receives a real-or-disqualified input.

## The Integrity-Gate for "Exceptional Initial Outburst → Scale"

The most dangerous moment is precisely the one the owner names: an **exceptional initial
outburst** — a huge first-call spike — tempting an immediate **Scale-grade commitment** (scale
inventory, spend, retail). This is where amplification is *most* likely (big spikes are what
amplification buys) **and** the commitment is *most* irreversible. The calling sequence already
says the first call **defaults to transient** and acts **in-window**; this gate governs whether
that in-window action may be **material/irreversible** (= "Scale").

**Vocabulary note (mint nothing):** "Scale" is not Orca vocabulary; it maps to a **material /
irreversible commitment** — Commit-grade on the operator ladder (Excluded→Watch→Probe→Test→Hold→
**Move**→**Commit**), i.e. a costly `act / phase / narrow / costly-defend` at **long horizon**
in the C3 ceiling vocabulary `{act, phase, narrow, hold, defend}`. The gate is a *placement* of
three existing rules onto the outburst case, not a new ceiling.

**The gate (fuses three ratified rules):** an exceptional initial outburst may authorize a
material / Scale-grade commitment **only if all three hold** —

1. **Manufactured axis CLEARED by a RUN discriminator, not an assumption** (R3d unlock rule:
   "a material/irreversible action earns a durable-grade green-light only by *running* the
   discriminator"). An outburst with unrun clearing-checks is **R3b unconfirmed**, not clean.
2. **≥2 independent converging origins of costly behavior** (buyer-proof AR-02), and —
3. **those origins sit OUTSIDE any single coordinated/amplified layer** (the manufactured-defeater
   + the strip-test): the costly behavior must survive stripping the amplified layer, or the
   "only costly signal inside the coordinated layer" defeater fires.

**Default posture when the gate is not cleared — highest EV, not reflexive caution.** The goal is
the **highest-expected-value action**, not zero manufactured exposure — so an unconfirmed outburst
is **not** reflexively shrunk to a timid token. It takes the **largest +EV *recoverable* move** the
breakout warrants (a recoverable position can still be aggressive — that is how a genuine breakout
under hype is *captured*, not missed). What the unmet gate withholds is only the **irreversible /
ruinous** escalation (the ≥2-origin Scale-grade step): there, highest-EV is itself **ruin-averse**
(an absorbing, bet-the-farm downside makes the long-run EV negative even when the one-shot bet looks
positive — you do not Scale-commit irreversibly on an unverified spike). **Irreversibility must be a
demonstrable property of the *action*** (a sunk, unrecoverable commitment you cannot unwind) — **not a
synonym for our uncertainty about the demand.** Being *unsure* a spike is real does **not** make the
action irreversible, and uncertainty **may not be relabeled as irreversibility to justify caution**.
Demand uncertainty **sizes** the recoverable bet (an EV input); only the action's own **provable**
unrecoverability triggers the ruin floor. The **least-recoverable dimension binds** (economic,
reputational, operational, evidence-contamination; R3b anti-slicing — "a small test that manufactures
the signal it would read is material, not reversible"). **Manufactured risk is an EV *input* — it
lowers the expected payoff and raises the downside odds — not a *veto*.** (Qualitative posture: the
**best action on all available current evidence** — the spine's measurement target, right-sized action
under evidence constraints — reasoned per-case, **not** a computed `P×payoff` model. INV-1 holds; owner
confirmed qualitative, not numeric.)

**Why this delivers low-FP and low-FN simultaneously** (the two-sided promise):
- **Low false-positive:** you do **not** Scale into bucket-(2) on a spike whose costly behavior is
  all inside the paid layer. The strictest bar applies exactly where manufacturing is most likely.
- **Low false-negative (the breakout half — load-bearing):** hype does **not** freeze you. You take
  the **largest +EV recoverable position** in-window (not a token), and you **monitor to earn the
  irreversible upgrade** as independent costly behavior persists — bucket (3) is *captured*, just
  *earned* rather than *assumed*. **Seed-then-real is explicit:** a manufactured *start* is **not a
  permanent disqualification** — amplification can ignite real demand, so a seeded spike that
  *consolidates* into real costly behavior earns its way up the ceiling rather than being discounted
  forever. **Missing a real breakout is a real cost the EV weighs**, not a free default; running the
  discriminator is the unlock for the irreversible step.

## Honest Limits (what is undetectable; why forward/live; the arms-race)

- **Forward/live capability — NOT backtestable.** Backtests cannot recover historical paid-proxy
  spend: who paid whom, deleted sponsorships, private coordination, since-scrubbed seeding. The
  durability probe tests the **persistence** axis, not manufactured-separation (thesis Central Read
  #5; taxonomy Layer 5). **Cross-signal consistency is at best a *partial* historical flag and is
  claimed only at that strength.** No manufactured/real claim is backtest-validated; this entire
  competency caps at the **forward/live** posture and at `product_learning` evidence.
- **Genuinely undetectable from public signals (name them; never assert "clean"):**
  - **Paid genuine-purchase reviews/UGC** — real money, real purchase, paid opinion. No fabrication
    tell fires.
  - **Sophisticated bot/swarm farms** — aged accounts + residential proxies + LLM-varied text
    defeat the account/timing/copied-phrase tells.
  - **Private coordination** — Discord/DM brigading, engagement pods — with public-looking,
    independent-looking output.
  - **Undisclosed paid promotion with no public ad footprint** (dark posts, whitelisting).
  - **Insider channel-stuffing** where sell-in is invisible and only sell-through is observable.
  - Honest move for all of these: a **confirmed-absent** disposition requires a clearing-check that
    was actually *run and passed*; where no clearing-check is possible, the risk is a **standing
    inherent-limit cap** (R3e) — advise the recoverable path, **do not** upgrade to "clean."
- **Outside-in capability gap (folded from the external research).** The *strongest* detection
  evidence is largely **not available to an outside-in reader**: reimbursement/broker records and
  internal documents are off-platform or private; the high-value **shared-reviewer network** signal
  needs a large collected corpus of reviewer histories (**capture-gated** — now scoped to a
  **within-category** ego-graph and spec'd in
  `orca/product/spines/capture/source_families/retail_pdp/retail_pdp_review_capture_spec_v0.md`;
  cross-industry remains out of scope, and within-category overlap is noisier so it corroborates
  rather than verdicts alone);
  and the proprietary integrity tools (Bazaarvoice, PowerReviews) rely on **first-party metadata**
  (IP, device reputation, submission velocity) Orca does not have. Public creator-audit tools
  (HypeAuditor, Modash) are **screening, not proof**; Botometer X is limited to pre-2023 archives;
  Fakespot was discontinued (2025). Orca therefore works mostly with the **moderate** and
  **weak-alone** public tells — *plus* the network graph where capture allows — and **consumes** the
  near-dispositive Tier-1 evidence (a regulator action, a whistleblower post) **when it already
  exists**, but cannot manufacture it. This is why a confident read leans on **convergence** and on
  **costly-behavior-under-the-layer**, not on any single high-reliability detector.
- **Confirmed-absent ≠ positive.** Clearing the manufactured risk earns the **neutral baseline**,
  never positive demand credit (anti-gaming; AR-05 / R3c).
- **The evasion arms-race.** Every tell, once known, is evadable, and amplification sophistication
  rises with the stakes. The defense is **not a fixed tell-list** (which rots fastest exactly where
  it is honor-maintained) but three durable principles: **(a) independence/convergence** (expensive
  to fake across many independent venue families at once); **(b) costly-behavior-under-the-layer**
  (the strip-test — manufacturing real repeat/switching/sellout across independent venues is the
  expensive thing amplifiers avoid); **(c) a sharpenable judgment competency over cases**, not a
  solved threshold (the discipline note's explicit posture). The arms-race is **won on the
  cost-asymmetry, not on out-listing the adversary.**
- **Highest-EV posture, not reflexive caution (the asymmetry is ruin-shaped, not fake-shaped).** The
  read optimizes for the **highest-EV action**, not for zero manufactured exposure. It leans **into**
  +EV recoverable breakout bets under manufactured uncertainty (missing a real breakout is a real
  cost — and a manufactured start can consolidate into real demand); it stays conservative **only**
  as the bet becomes **demonstrably irreversible / ruinous** (a *provable* property of the action,
  **not** mere demand-uncertainty — where highest EV is itself ruin-averse). Manufactured
  risk is an **EV input, not a veto**; verification is the unlock that opens the irreversible step.
  (Qualitative — INV-1; no EV score.)
- **Reimbursement / paid-genuine-purchase — settled at the demand and action layer, not the review
  layer.** The hardest case (verified + real-purchase + non-templated from aged accounts → no
  review-level tell fires) and it **is** the dangerous middle. It is **not** settled by a better
  review detector (none exists from public data); it is settled by what reimbursement **cannot**
  fix: **(a)** the within-category **co-review roster graph** — it cleans each review but not roster
  reuse, unless the broker fully partitions the pool (the expensive evasion that shrinks the
  operation); **(b)** **persistence over a longitudinal corpus** — it buys a *burst*, not sustained
  independent costly behavior (the Amazon cohort reverted in weeks); this needs the forward-captured
  time series, which is unbacktestable; and **(c)** the **EV-sized action posture** — an
  undetectable-but-unconfirmed burst still earns the largest +EV *recoverable* bet (you do not miss
  the breakout), but not the irreversible Scale step until it's earned. Verified-purchase
  share remains a **low-side flag, never a high-side clearance**. Honest ceiling: an operator who
  *subsidizes real repeat purchase across venues for months* defeats all three — but that costs ~
  what real demand costs, so **it reads as real demand and is not chased** (you are getting real
  repeat purchase; the normal persistence monitor catches a later collapse if the subsidy ends).
  The cost-asymmetry endgame, not a free win.
