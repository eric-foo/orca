# Participant Packet

## Decision Context

Joah Beauty is Kiss Products' K-beauty line, positioned as an accessible, mass-channel
alternative to prestige K-beauty brands. Its primary retail distribution at the time of this
decision is CVS Pharmacy, with roughly 4,000 doors nationwide. The mass-retail channel was a
deliberate strategic bet: CVS provides scale, physical discovery, and reach into a broad
consumer base that might not shop specialty or DTC-first beauty. The brand also operates a
Shopify DTC storefront (joahbeauty.com), which functions as a brand-building and
direct-sales channel alongside the retail footprint.

The strategic tension in mid-2024 is whether the CVS channel commitment is delivering
proportionate returns relative to its costs — including slotting, promotional compliance,
logistics, planogram constraints, and markdown risk — or whether the brand's energy and
investment would be better directed toward DTC growth, channel diversification, or a
reduced-door retail footprint. The beauty mass-retail environment at this period involved
category pressure from shelf resets, competition from both heritage mass brands and K-beauty
entrants, and ongoing questions about whether exclusive single-retailer mass plays limit
brand equity development. The DTC channel, by contrast, offers margin, data ownership, and
positioning flexibility, but at lower scale.

The evidence set available for this decision consists entirely of public DTC site captures
(Wayback Machine snapshots of joahbeauty.com pages) taken between April 2023 and May 2024.
These captures reflect what the brand was communicating to consumers through its owned
storefront: product assortment, promotional behavior, brand narrative, experiential
activations, and digital acquisition channels. What is not present is any direct CVS
sell-through data, door-level velocity, retailer feedback, or competitive shelf data. The
decision must be made from the DTC-side signal alone, with full awareness that the absence
of retail performance data is a material constraint on the quality of inference.

## Known Uncertainties

- CVS sell-through data, door-level velocity, reorder cadence, and markdown rates are
  entirely absent from the evidence set; the most direct signal for evaluating the retail
  channel's performance cannot be observed from these captures.
- No competitive benchmarking is present — neither CVS shelf composition nor the relative
  performance of comparable K-beauty or mass-beauty brands is visible in the evidence.
- Snapshot dating introduces representation risk: pages captured at a single point in time
  may not reflect what was live at the decision date, and some captures (E7, E8, E11) were
  taken approximately 6 weeks before the cutoff.
- Social proof signals — consumer reviews, ratings, UGC volume, social media engagement —
  are not captured in the evidence set; sentiment and organic demand indicators must be
  inferred indirectly, if at all, from DTC merchandising choices.
