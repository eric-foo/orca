# Participant Packet

## Decision Context

Sundots, operating at sundots.com as of late 2018, was a direct-to-consumer brand built
around a single product category: ingestible gummy supplements marketed for UV-protection
benefits. The brand's positioning rested on the premise that certain dietary compounds —
most prominently fern extract (Polypodium leucotomos) — could support the skin's natural
defenses against UV damage when taken orally. This was a narrow and scientifically specific
niche: not sunscreen, not general wellness, but an ingestible supplement making
UV-protection-adjacent claims. The brand's identity, copywriting, naming convention, and
community messaging were all anchored to this UV-protection frame at the time of this
evidence window (April through September 2018).

The strategic question facing the founder as of the 2018-10-01 cutoff is whether this
UV-specific positioning represents a durable, scalable brand foundation or a ceiling. The
UV-ingestible niche offers clarity and differentiation, but it is inherently seasonal,
carries meaningful regulatory scrutiny around efficacy claims, and may constrain total
addressable market relative to a broader skin-health gummy platform. A pivot to a broader
skin-health positioning would potentially open adjacencies in collagen, antioxidant,
hydration, and general skin wellness categories that were seeing consumer momentum in the
2017–2018 DTC wellness wave. However, a rename and repositioning at this stage would mean
writing off existing brand equity, resetting community expectations, and absorbing the
execution risk of a category expansion before the original positioning had been fully
validated at scale.

The evidence available for this decision is narrow: six snapshots of the Sundots web
presence between April 2018 and September 2018 (five homepage snapshots and one FAQ page).
There are no sales figures, conversion data, press mentions, investor materials, or consumer
research materials in the evidence set. The judgment must rely entirely on what the brand's
own public-facing materials reveal about positioning evolution, messaging stability or drift,
product breadth signals, and any early indicators of strategic tension between the
UV-specific frame and a potentially broader skin-health aspiration.
