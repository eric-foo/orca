# Participant Packet — Enriched v0

## Decision Context

You are advising **Topicals** as of **15 March 2021**. Topicals is a young direct-to-consumer skincare brand, roughly seven months past its launch, currently selling through its own online store and one national department-store retail partner (Nordstrom). The live question is **the best way forward for its near-term distribution and growth strategy** — specifically **whether and how to make a major retail/wholesale expansion move beyond direct-to-consumer**, for example pursuing a broad national beauty-retail account that would put the brand on wider physical and retail-partner shelves. The options span **watch / hold / pursue a limited or pilot retail entry / commit to a broad retail expansion**. The packet presents only what was knowable on or before the cutoff. Decide using only the information in this brief — not outside or later knowledge.

## Evidence Units

**Frame / context (stated premises):**
- `E1` — **Company/brand:** Topicals, founded 2020 (Olamide Olowe, Claudia Teng); DTC skincare for chronic skin conditions.
- `E2` — **Channel state at cutoff:** launched 2020-08-07; selling via own DTC site + Nordstrom; ~7 months past launch. (Nordstrom presence independently corroborated by E11.)
- `E3` — **Expansion opportunity:** a broad national beauty-retail account is an available next-stage path, distinct from the current DTC + single-retailer setup.
- `E4` — DTC→broader wholesale **raises operational demands** (inventory, supply chain, demand planning, vendor terms) — a recognized commitment/execution-risk lever (general base rate).
- `E5` — **Comparable DTC-to-retail expansions** exist across the market (general base rate).

## Known Uncertainties

- The strongest demand-pressure signals (review volume on `E7`/`E8`, sold-out states) come from the **brand's own site**; weigh how much source-controlled signal can carry.
- The one clearly **independent** origin (`E11`, Nordstrom) confirms *placement* but carries **no independent demand-volume** data; independent costly-behavior (third-party reviews, community) is **not present** in this packet.
- `E9` (press) and `E10` (influencers) establish only that the brand had *some* press section and *no* substantive influencer roster on its own surface; neither quantifies independent attention.
- There is no pre-cutoff measure of Topicals' own **operational readiness** to absorb a broad wholesale expansion (`E4` names the risk; nothing here measures it).
- Whether the available evidence is enough to *decide* the timing and breadth of a broad retail move in advance — versus only being legible in hindsight — is the crux.
