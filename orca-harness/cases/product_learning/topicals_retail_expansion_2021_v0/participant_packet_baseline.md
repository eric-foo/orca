# Participant Packet

## Decision Context

You are advising Topicals as of **15 March 2021**. Topicals is a young direct-to-consumer skincare brand, roughly seven months past its launch, currently selling through its own online store and one retail partner. The live question is **the best way forward for its near-term distribution and growth strategy** — specifically **whether and how to make a major retail/wholesale expansion move beyond direct-to-consumer**, for example pursuing a national beauty-retail account that would put the brand on wider physical and retail-partner shelves. The packet is intentionally narrow: it presents only what was knowable on or before the cutoff (15 March 2021). The options span watch / hold / pursue a limited or pilot retail entry / commit to a broad retail expansion. Decide using only the information in this brief — not outside or later knowledge.

## Evidence Units

- `E1`: **Company/brand** — Topicals was founded in 2020 by Olamide Olowe and Claudia Teng as a direct-to-consumer skincare brand focused on products for chronic skin conditions.
- `E2`: **Channel state at the cutoff** — Topicals launched on 2020-08-07, selling online through its own DTC site and through Nordstrom. At the cutoff it is an early-stage brand roughly seven months past launch.
- `E3`: **Expansion opportunity** — for a young DTC beauty brand, moving into a national beauty-retail account (a wholesale shelf-presence relationship) is an available next-stage distribution path, distinct from the existing DTC channel.
- `E4`: Moving from DTC into wholesale/retail distribution **raises operational demands** — inventory, supply chain, demand planning, and vendor terms — and is a recognized commitment/execution-risk growth lever for an early-stage brand (general base rate).
- `E5`: **Comparable DTC-to-retail expansions** exist across the consumer/beauty market (general base rate).

## Known Uncertainties

- The evidence shows a national retail expansion is an available growth path **and** that it raises real operational demands and commitment cost for an early-stage brand; it does not settle whether now is the right time or how broad the move should be.
- There is no pre-cutoff measurement of *Topicals' own* operational readiness to support a wholesale expansion (inventory depth, supply-chain capacity, demand planning) in this packet.
- Whether the general base rates (E4, E5) are enough to *decide* the timing and breadth of the move in advance — versus only being legible in hindsight — is the crux of this decision.
