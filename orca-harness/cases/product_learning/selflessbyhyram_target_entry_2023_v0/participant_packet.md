## Decision Context

Selfless by Hyram is a skincare brand founded by Hyram Yarbro, a beauty content creator
with a large social media following. The brand launched as a DTC-first operation, selling
serums, moisturizers, SPF products, and multi-step routine bundles directly through its own
website. The brand's identity is built on its founder's personal voice, ingredient
transparency, and accessibility rhetoric — the name "Selfless" signals community-oriented
values, and the product line is positioned around inclusivity and approachability rather
than luxury exclusivity. The DTC channel is not merely a logistics choice; it reflects the
influencer-origin model in which the founder's credibility and direct relationship with
followers is the primary demand-generation mechanism.

The strategic tension at the decision date is whether to leverage that demand base by
expanding into Target distribution at a repriced tier, or to protect the DTC-first model by
declining or deferring mass-retail entry. Entering Target would expand physical retail reach
and potentially drive volume, but would require repricing some SKUs below current DTC list
prices, creating visible price-tier dissonance that existing DTC customers could observe.
It would also subject the brand to the shelf and margin dynamics of a large retail buyer,
reducing direct customer relationship control. Maintaining DTC-first preserves pricing
integrity, community trust, and brand narrative coherence but foregoes the distribution
scale and discovery surface that Target placement would provide.

The evidence available to this decision is bounded entirely to pre-cutoff public captures of
the brand's own DTC site — homepage snapshots across approximately ten months, a full catalog
capture, an about-us page, and a curated routine collection page. No third-party retail data,
sell-through figures, social listening data, competitor channel histories, or Target
negotiation terms are available in this packet. Recommendations should be calibrated to this
evidence scope and should not import any knowledge of what the brand actually did after
2023-02-01.
