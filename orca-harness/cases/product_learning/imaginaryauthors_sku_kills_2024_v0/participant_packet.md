# Participant Packet

## Decision Context

Imaginary Authors is a small Portland, Oregon-based independent fragrance brand founded by
perfumer Josh Meyer. The label occupies a distinct niche within artisan perfumery: each
fragrance is conceived as an olfactory novel — packaged with a fictional author attribution,
a narrative premise, and writing that situates the scent in an invented literary world.
Prices run roughly $50–$130 depending on size and format. The brand sells direct-to-consumer
via its Shopify storefront and through a limited number of retail partners. Its customers
are enthusiast and niche-fragrance buyers who follow the label closely, discuss individual
releases on community forums, and develop strong attachment to specific titles. For this
customer segment, the catalog functions less like a product menu and more like a curated
body of work: additions and removals carry symbolic weight beyond their commercial
contribution.

The portfolio-management tension the decision surfaces is a familiar one for small-batch
specialty brands: maintaining a full lineup preserves customer optionality, signals catalog
permanence, and avoids the community friction that comes with perceived discontinuations;
pruning underperforming SKUs reduces production complexity, frees working capital, sharpens
the narrative focus of the lineup, and concentrates marketing energy on titles with stronger
velocity. For a brand at Imaginary Authors' scale, these tradeoffs are not symmetric.
Enthusiast customers notice discontinuations and discuss them publicly; quiet removals from
a DTC storefront are rarely quiet in practice. At the same time, carrying slow-moving
inventory indefinitely has real costs. The question is whether the evidence on hand is
sufficient to justify crossing that threshold for Whispered Myths and Telegrama specifically.

This packet contains only what was publicly observable via the DTC storefront on or before
the decision cutoff of 2024-08-01. No internal sales data, no wholesale volume, no margin
information, and no community sentiment data are available. The evidence consists of Wayback
Machine archive captures of the imaginaryauthors.com homepage at two points in time (one
cutoff-proximate, one approximately 14 months earlier) and individual product-page captures
for the two candidate SKUs alongside the full catalog page. Decide using only what is in
this packet.

## Known Uncertainties

- **No internal sales data is available.** The packet contains no unit volume, revenue
  contribution, margin, or sell-through information for any SKU. Assessing whether Whispered
  Myths or Telegrama are genuinely underperforming relative to the rest of the lineup
  requires inference from public signals only, and public DTC storefronts carry limited
  velocity signal.

- **Snapshot timing is uneven across evidence units.** E1 is cutoff-proximate (July 2024);
  E3 is six weeks pre-cutoff (June 2024); E4 and E5 are five months pre-cutoff (March 2024);
  E2 is 14 months pre-cutoff (May 2023). Changes to product status, pricing, or catalog
  composition between March 2024 and the August 2024 cutoff are not directly captured in the
  most granular evidence units.

- **No community sentiment data is included.** Enthusiast-community forums, Reddit
  discussions, and fragrance-review sites are relevant channels for assessing product
  attachment in this brand's customer segment. None of these sources are in the packet;
  the decision must be made without direct evidence of how customers regard these specific
  titles.

- **Absence from prominent placement is not evidence of low performance.** Products that are
  not featured on the homepage may be slow-movers, or they may simply not have been selected
  for promotional rotation during the snapshot window. The packet cannot distinguish between
  these explanations from public evidence alone.
